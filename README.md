## üåç Overview ‚Äî Turkey Trip

Turkey is a country with an exceptionally rich and layered history. From the **Hittites**, **Ancient Greeks**, and the **Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire**, to the long-lasting **Ottoman Empire**, the land of **Anatolia** preserves traces of multiple civilizations.  
Across this region, one can experience everything from **Mediterranean coastal landscapes** to the **ancient grandeur of Ottoman cities**, with historical ruins scattered throughout the land. There is simply too much worth visiting.

Popular destinations include **Istanbul, Izmir, Bursa, Fethiye, Antalya, Pamukkale, and Cappadocia**.

Due to time constraints, our itinerary was relatively tight. We chose to focus on three representative regions:
- **Istanbul** ‚Äî a historical metropolis bridging Europe and Asia  
- **Cappadocia** ‚Äî famous for its unique landscapes and hot air balloon experience  
- **Antalya** ‚Äî a Mediterranean city combining ancient ruins with coastal scenery  

### Practical Notes
- **Visa**: Turkey offers an **e-visa** system. The official website requires only an online form and payment. Approval is almost instant. No supporting documents (such as flight tickets or hotel bookings) are required.  
- **Currency & Payment**: It is recommended to bring **USD or EUR** to exchange for Turkish Lira. **WeChat Pay and Alipay are generally not accepted**. Credit cards are widely accepted in most places; **HSBC cards offer very competitive exchange rates**, often better than cash.  
- **Transportation**: Domestic flights within Turkey are convenient and relatively inexpensive. Long-distance buses are also a viable and affordable option.
